    Often as low as $5 to $25, to open a savings account, which is typically a prerequisite for membership.

  • What is the rate of interest?

    Typically range from 0.05% to 1.00% APY (Annual Percentage Yield), though some credit unions may offer higher rates for larger balances or special accounts.
    Rates vary based on the term length and the credit union, typically ranging from 1.00% to 4.00% APY for terms from 6 months to 5 years. Features: CDs typically offer higher interest rates than regular savings or money market accounts in exchange for keeping your funds deposited for a fixed period.

  • When will I receive my account statement?

    Typical for: Checking accounts, savings accounts, money market accounts, and credit cards.

    When: Statements are generally issued at the end of each month. You can expect to receive them within the first week of the new month.

    Example: If your statement cycle ends on June 30, you should receive your statement by early July.
